DOs of Digital Literacy

Use strong passwords
Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols to keep accounts safe.
Verify information before sharing
Always check if news or information is from a trusted source to avoid spreading misinformation.
Protect personal information
Do not share sensitive details like OTPs, passwords, or bank information online.
Keep software updated
Regular updates help protect devices from viruses and security threats.
Use secure websites
Look for “HTTPS” in the URL before entering personal data.
Be respectful online (Digital Etiquette)
Communicate politely and avoid cyberbullying or offensive language.
Backup important data
Save copies of files in cloud storage or external devices.
Be aware of privacy settings
Adjust settings on social media to control who can see your information.


❌ DON’Ts of Digital Literacy

Don’t share passwords with anyone
Even friends should not have access to your private accounts.
Don’t click on suspicious links
Unknown links may contain malware or phishing scams.
Don’t download from untrusted sources
It can harm your device with viruses.
Don’t overshare on social media
Avoid posting personal details like location, phone number, etc.
Don’t believe everything online
Fake news and misinformation are common.
Don’t ignore cybersecurity warnings
Alerts about unsafe websites or files should be taken seriously.
Don’t use public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions
It may not be secure for banking or confidential work.
Don’t engage in cyberbullying
Respect others and report harmful behavior.
